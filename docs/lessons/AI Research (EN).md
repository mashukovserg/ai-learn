# AI Research: AI for Research — The Technical Manifesto

**Level:** Advanced  
**Duration:** 3h

## Chapter 1: The Research Bottleneck — Volume Crisis

The world of science is in a state of informational overheating. Thousands of papers are published every day in narrow fields of knowledge. No living human is capable of tracking this flow in real-time. This leads to a "research bottleneck": we spend time on duplicate discoveries or developing ideas that have already been debunked in related disciplines. An AI researcher is not just a chatbot; it is an autonomous system of filtration and synthesis that turns an ocean of data into a structured knowledge map.

We are moving from passive searches in Google Scholar to active agentic modeling. An agent doesn't just find links; it builds arguments, looks for contradictions in methodology, and identifies "blind spots" where research has not yet been conducted.

## Chapter 2: Automated Search and the PRISMA Protocol

Modern AI researchers operate according to strict academic protocols. In this chapter, we explore how to teach agents:
1. **Query Expansion:** Turning a single idea into dozens of technical queries in different languages and using synonyms.
2. **Semantic Reranking:** Using embeddings to evaluate a paper's relevance based on its abstract, filtering out up to 95% of noise at the input.
3. **Automated Extraction:** Using PDF parsing tools to isolate only the core information: sample size, accuracy, method, and limitations.

## Chapter 3: Recursive Summarization (The Snowball Method)

The context window problem prevents us from reading 100 papers at once. We use the "Snowball" method.
* **Atomic Stage:** Compressing each paper into a set of hard facts.
* **Cluster Stage:** Grouping facts by theme (e.g., "Ecological Impact" vs. "Economic Benefit").
* **Synthetic Stage:** Writing the final report based on compressed and verified data.
This approach preserves details that are usually lost during simple text summarization.

## Chapter 4: Data Auditing via Code Interpreter

One of an agent's most powerful functions is the ability to double-check calculations. An AI lab assistant extracts raw data from tables within a PDF, writes a Python script, and re-runs the statistical tests. We discuss cases where AI found mathematical errors in peer-reviewed papers. This makes science more transparent and reliable.

## Chapter 5: Citation Discipline and DOI Verification

Hallucinations in references are a disaster for any research. We implement the "Verify then Trust" rule. Every agent claim must pass through a database verification stage. If a DOI (unique paper identifier) is not found in the Crossref or Semantic Scholar database, the claim is considered unreliable and removed. This transforms the AI into a responsible co-author.

## Chapter 6: Ethics and the Future of Co-authorship

How should AI be credited in an author list? The 2025 consensus states: AI cannot be an author but must be declared as an analytical tool. We discuss the boundaries of human responsibility and the role of the "Human-in-the-loop," without which the final conclusion remains merely a statistical probability.

## Conclusion

You are on the threshold of a new era of research. Using AI agents allows us to see further and deeper than ever before. Mastering the art of conducting this process is the key to breakthroughs in any field of knowledge.
